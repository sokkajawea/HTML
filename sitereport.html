Portfolio Page

The portfolio page started off as a list of projects I worked on rather than a grid format of them. The initial plan was to have the list headings link to the projects instead of having any pictures at all. I wanted to have a very short and quick introduction to the webpage before I presented the projects and moved on so I added a heading and a one line sentence about the contents of the page. I got the idea of the portfolio structure and hover animations from Riccardo Zanutta's Work page linked below:

http://riccardozanutta.com/?ref=hackernoon.com

Like his portfolio, I wanted to have a good hover effect when the images were hovered over in order to make it more dynamic and interactive for the user. I started off with a simple 1 picture per row but it looked so empty, so I proceeded to make it 4 in a row and have 2 rows, each with a hovering picture which shows a brief description of the project I worked on. I wanted to also remain consistent with the background image and the navigation format so I kept them the same, however, in this page was the beginning of the footer of every page which had undergone several changes from the start to the final edit. It started off as just the icons on the bottom right of the page and it slowly evolved to being in the center of the bottom of the page. It looked bland at first which led me to add a background to it which complimented the black of the motherboard background of the whole page. With the hover effect, I wanted the image to be overlayed with a grey background where you can see the picture but also have the sense of selection. I decided that the best way to have the text appear on the overlay was to have it slide up from the bottom, which was the most appealing way to show animation to the eye. The the curved borders were a no brainer as the rectangular shape of the images and overlay looked so plain and basic whereas I wanted the boxes to look dynamic and smooth.

Contact Page

With the contact page, it was genuinely just a matter of formatting because the page is basically just a form below a heading which needs to open in the user's default mail software/browser. I decided on the required fields to be the first name, surname and email address- these would be used in order for me to contact the visitor easily and quicker. I added a relatively large text area on the form table in order to give the visitor as much space as they need for a message.

For this page, there was no inspiration but the basic contact form you'd see anywhere. I wanted to have both labels and placeholders so it becomes easier to know what you're entering and where because many times, I personally got confused by them. The submit button looked off place when in one column so i made it spread across the 2 columns I have for the form and had the classic green color as the background. I had stars (*) reference the fact that the input was required and placed that sentence/statement just above the footer on the left hand side - I also wanted to have the asterisks red to pop them out.

Blog page

With the final page of the website, as always, I wanted to stay consistent with the navigation bar and the background. However, this page took so much longer because the only reference for a blog that I had in my head was really outdated and I also never really read nor visited a blog before. So, naturally, I resorted to the internet and looked around which resulted in my inspiration for the whole page; Florin Pop's Blog Page on his website. Below, you can find his blog webpage:

https://www.florin-pop.com/blog/

Florin's blog had a nice hover effect that I wanted to use minus the added shadows on his blog entry boxes. Unlike his, my entries are not clickable so they don't lead to places whereas his blog posts are anchored to a new page where he elaborates on the subject further and further. The whole theme of my blog page is an entry per eventful session like making a new page or major changes to existing pages, and that way the page looks more organized in chronological order. The hover effect gave it a more dynamic look on the entries but also gave it a less boring shape to the page.
